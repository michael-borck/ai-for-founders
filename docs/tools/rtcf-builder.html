Before and After

Weak Prompt:
"Is my startup idea any good?"
RTCF Prompt:
"You are a skeptical seed investor who has seen 500 pitches (R). Find the three weakest assumptions in my idea and the one that would kill it (T). The idea: a booking app for solo tradespeople in WA, pre-revenue, two founders, no funding yet (C). Present as a short numbered list, each weakness with one question I must answer before I build (F)."
RTCF in Practice
• You don't need all 4 elements every time --- use what's relevant
• RTCF is your conversation starter, not your final answer
• Follow up with refinements: "Make it more specific" or "Add industry context"
• Think of AI as a capable colleague, not a magic tool
• The best prompts invite conversation, not just commands

Advanced Conversation Techniques

Reverse Prompting

Let AI interview you to build context before giving recommendations. Surfaces assumptions you didn't know you had.

I'm launching a booking app for solo tradespeople. Before giving me any advice, interview me first. Ask me questions one at a time to understand my specific context, constraints, and goals. Start with your first question.

Multi-Perspective Analysis

Get honest takes from different roles on your decision. Catches blind spots.

I'm weighing [your decision]. Act as my advisory panel and analyse this from multiple perspectives: 1. As a cautious CFO: does the money work? 2. As a skeptical customer: why would I not buy? 3. As a growth marketer: how would we reach people? 4. As an operator: what breaks at scale? For each, give me 3-4 key points.

Devil's Advocate

Force AI to argue against your position. Overcomes confirmation bias and reveals hidden assumptions.

I'm planning to [your plan]. Play devil's advocate. Give me the strongest possible arguments against this. Be harsh but fair. I want to know: 1. Why might this fail? 2. What am I probably not thinking about? 3. What alternative explanations exist? 4. What would a skeptical investor say? Don't hold back.

Conversational Data Analysis

Upload data and explore it through conversation rather than a single prompt.

I've uploaded a [type] dataset from [your business]. What patterns do you see? Give me an initial overview of the data. [Then follow up with:] - Why might [observation] occur? - Are there any unexpected trends? - Create a visualisation of [specific aspect] - What should I check before I trust this?

VET Framework Check

Use after any AI interaction to push back and verify outputs.

You just told me [AI's claim]. Help me VET this: - VERIFY: What sources support this claim? Can I find this independently? - EXPLAIN: Can you break this down so I can explain it in my own words? - TEST: What edge cases or conditions might make this wrong?

Source Synthesis (NotebookLM)

Based on the uploaded documents: 1. What are the main points across these documents? 2. Where do they disagree? 3. What gaps or open questions remain? 4. What assumptions do they share? 5. Suggest 3 questions I should dig into next.
