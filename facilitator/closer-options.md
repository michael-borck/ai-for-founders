# The Ben fork: how to close the hour

**The decision:** does the 60-minute session end on pure live work, or trade 5 minutes of live depth for a "horizon" showcase (NotebookLM / Teachable Machine) that shows where AI is heading?

This is the one real choice worth Ben's time. Everything else is settled.

---

## The spine has no slack

The 60 minutes are already fully allocated:

| Min | Block |
|---|---|
| 0–5 | Hook + thesis |
| 5–12 | The two traps |
| 12–45 | LIVE: one idea, three moves |
| 45–53 | The trust tool |
| 53–58 | The method, distilled |
| 58–60 | Takeaways |

**A 5-minute closer is not free — it costs 5 minutes of live depth.** That is the real tradeoff, and it's the only thing to decide.

---

## Option A — Pure live (60 min)

Every minute on the volunteer's idea through the three moves, ending on the method + takeaways. The horizon is mentioned only as a take-home nudge ("tonight, drop your one-pager into NotebookLM — free — and generate a podcast of your own idea").

- **Pro:** maximum thesis depth; ends on the active method ("you direct it, don't delegate it"); zero live-demo risk.
- **Con:** no visceral "press button → podcast" moment in the room; the "where it's heading" promise is homework, not spectacle.

## Option B — Live (55 min) + NotebookLM closer (5 min)

Cut 5 minutes from the live work (recommend: trim the traps to **one** named live — Sycophancy — since move 1 demonstrates it anyway; Gell-Mann gets a 30-second mention). Use the freed 5 minutes for a live NotebookLM showcase on the volunteer's one-pager.

- **Pro:** the horizon lands live and is genuinely memorable; NotebookLM is *grounded RAG* (it sticks to the founder's document, not the average) — which is quietly on-thesis (the "precise" column of the trust tool vs free chat's "average").
- **Con:** 5 minutes less depth; a context switch to a new tool; passive (attendees watch you); ends on a button-press, slightly undercutting "thinking partner, not stand-in" if it's the final beat.

### If B: what the closer shows

- **NotebookLM (recommended closer):** paste the volunteer's idea as a source → generate a **Briefing Doc**, **FAQ**, or the two-person **Audio Overview (podcast)** in ~60 seconds. Frame it as: *"This isn't the average — it's grounded in her document. Same model family, different placement on the trust tool."* Most founder-relevant; most reliable.
- **Teachable Machine (alternative closer):** train an image classifier live in 90 seconds (e.g., "tool vs. receipt"). Shows working ML from scratch — the "anyone can build this" beat. Less founder-relevant, more "how the sausage is made."

## Option C — the middle ground (free)

**Pure live (A), but make the take-home horizon nudge concrete.** Add to slide 11 / the takeaways: a one-line, screen-shared glimpse of a pre-generated NotebookLM podcast *of the tradie idea* (no live generation, no time cost) — "this took 60 seconds to make from a one-pager; you can do it tonight." Attendees see the artefact without the demo eating 5 minutes.

---

## Recommendation

**Default to A (or C). Go B only if Ben specifically wants the horizon to land live — and fund it by cutting the traps to one.**

Reasoning: the blurb's load-bearing promise is "AI as a thinking partner rather than a stand-in." That promise is delivered by the live three-move work, not by a button-press closer. The closer is memorable but it is the most "stand-in"-flavoured moment in the session (press button, AI makes content for you). Ending there slightly undercuts the thesis. If the horizon must land in the room, put the closer **before** the method/takeaways (not as the finale), so the session still closes on the active method — but that still costs the 5 minutes.

If Ben is unsure: **C.** It costs nothing, keeps all 60 minutes on the thesis, and attendees still walk out knowing NotebookLM exists and what it can do to their idea tonight.

---

## Decision helper

- "I want maximum depth and a clean thesis landing." → **A**
- "I want attendees to gasp at where this is heading, and I'll cut the traps to fund it." → **B**
- "I want both, for free." → **C**
