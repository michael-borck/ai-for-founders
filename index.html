AI for Founders

Conversation, not delegation: a thinking partner you converse with, not a stand-in you hand the work to.

AI lets a team of one move like a team of ten, as long as you stay the one steering. This is the take-home from the session: a two-minute self-check, a prompting method, founder-ready prompts, and how to run it all privately on your own laptop.

The idea behind it

Where are you on your AI journey?

// two minutes, five questions

No right answers. This points you at the right next step, whether you have barely opened a chatbot or you are already building AI into your product.

    The method: RTCF

    // turn a vague prompt into a sharp one

    Most weak AI answers come from weak prompts. Give the model four things and the quality jumps. Dial Role up when you run a small or local model, and let it go light on a frontier model where the others carry the weight.

    Role

    Who should it be? "A skeptical seed investor."

    Task

    What exactly do you want done?

    Context

    Your situation, stage, constraints, audience.

    Format

    How should the answer come back?

    Vague
    Is my startup idea any good?
    RTCF
    Role: a skeptical seed investor who has seen 500 pitches.
Task: stress-test my idea and find the three weakest assumptions.
Context: a booking tool for solo tradespeople in WA, pre-revenue, two founders, no funding yet.
Format: a short list, each weakness with one question I should answer before I build.
    RTCF is a dial, not a recipe. The point is judgment: you decide what good looks like, the model just drafts toward it. It gets a full chapter in the free book, Conversation, Not Delegation.

    Run it private, on your own laptop

    // no subscription, no data leaving the machine
