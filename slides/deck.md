## Slide 1 — Hook

### AI for Founders: Where Your Edge Lives

> When a capability becomes universal, generic competence stops being an advantage. So where does your edge actually live?

*Show of hands: who's used AI on their idea this week?*

---

## Slide 2 — The thesis

A capability that drafts your copy, models your numbers, and rehearses your pitch can do the same for **every other founder in the room**.

Generic competence is the **baseline** — not the advantage.

The edge lives in your judgement: taste, context, and the variation no rival's identical model can reproduce.

---

## Slide 3 — Trap 1: Sycophancy

The model tells you what you want to hear.

Ask *"what do you think?"* → praise first, gentle critique second.

It's managing your feelings, not assessing your work.

**Defence:** prompt past it. *(We do this in move 2.)*

---

## Slide 4 — Trap 2: Gell-Mann Amnesia

You catch its errors in your own domain.

You trust it on the market, the regs, the numbers — the bits you can't check.

Smooth prose feels authoritative. **Smoothness ≠ accuracy.**

---

## Slide 5 — LIVE: one idea, three moves

Working in **free chat** — no subscription, nothing to install.

1. **Validate** — RTCF, two-pass
2. **Pressure-test** — past the flattery
3. **Sharpen the pitch** — two-pass on the pitch

*Run each on YOUR idea, in parallel with the volunteer's.*

---

## Slide 6 — Move 1: Validate (two-pass)

**Pass 1 (naive):** slick, generic, *everyone-got-this*.

**Pass 2 (with edge):** Role · Task · **Context** · Format.

> Context is where your edge enters. No model supplies it.

---

## Slide 7 — Move 2: Pressure-test

> Do not flatter me. Three reasons this fails. Name the load-bearing assumption.

Then: **Scale / Pivot / Kill** — and the cheapest experiment that moves it up a notch.

*Killing is a good outcome.*

---

## Slide 8 — Move 3: Sharpen the pitch

**Pass 1:** generic 60-word pitch. Forgettable.

**Pass 2:** in *your* voice, leading with the one thing only you would say.

---

## Slide 9 — The trust tool

**Average / Precise × Small / Large.**

- **Lean in:** competitor scans, brainstorming, first drafts.
- **Human owns it:** the go/no-go, the customer, the positioning call, any number in a pitch.

*Where it hurts to be wrong, you stay in charge.*

---

## Slide 10 — The method, in three sentences

1. **Add context** — the specifics only you hold.
2. **Prompt past the flattery** — ask "why does this fail?", not "what do you think?"
3. **Stay sceptical where you know least.**

> AI as a thinking partner, not a stand-in.
